Typhoon Bopha

Typhoon Bopha is the most powerful storm to hit the Philippines in 2012 that passed through the southern Philippines from Mindanao to Palawan, resulting in a significant loss of life and infrastructure, such us water supplies, and safe shelter. In the long term, further damage that affect public through the disruption of health system and leave without many access to health care.

Timeline of the Typhoon

Nov. 23
A weather system emerges in micronesia, but is perceived as having title chance of developing into a significant weather system.

Nov.30
Bopha is upgraded to a severe tropical storm. Few hours later typhoon bopha develops as its wind speed increases.

Dec. 2
Bopha strengthens to a super typhoon with wind speed that exceeds 150mph. Later it develops a double eyewalls as the whirling band of thunderstorm that surround the calmer eye increases. This is a sign that the the storm is intensifying.

Dec. 3
At 9 pm. Bopha makes landfall over Baganga, Mindanao, as a Category 5 super typhoon

Dec. 4 to 6
After raking acroos Davo oriental and Compostela valley provinces. Typhoon Bopha crosses into the southern and central regions of mindanao, downing power lines and triggering landslides.

Dec. 7 to 9
Bopha begins to strengthen again, but as it moves to the South China Sea and west of Palawan island province, it dissipates.
